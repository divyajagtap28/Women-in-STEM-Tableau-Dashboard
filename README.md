## Overview

This project analyzes gender representation across STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) fields using Tableau. Raw workforce and education data was cleaned and transformed in Excel, then modeled into an interactive dashboard covering occupational representation, graduation trends, education requirements, career-major alignment, and earnings by gender and ethnicity.

The goal was to move beyond a static chart and build a dashboard that supports genuine exploration — the kind a analyst or policy researcher could use to answer follow-up questions rather than just view a fixed snapshot.

## Objectives

- Quantify female representation across major STEM occupations
- Track STEM graduation trends by discipline from 2000–2015
- Compare minimum education requirements across STEM roles
- Measure how often graduates work in fields aligned with their major
- Compare median annual earnings by gender and ethnicity
- Package findings into a single, decision-ready dashboard

## Key Insights

**Representation**
- Biological Sciences shows the highest female representation among STEM occupations, while Engineering and Architecture remain comparatively underrepresented.

**Education Trends**
- Female graduation rates in Biological Sciences have risen steadily over the 2000–2015 period; Engineering and Computer Science show comparatively slower growth in female enrollment.

**Education Requirements**
- Healthcare-related STEM roles generally require higher formal qualifications, while Engineering and Computer Science show a more even distribution across education levels.

**Career Alignment**
- The share of graduates working in roles related to their major varies substantially by field, with some disciplines showing much stronger career-major alignment than others.

**Earnings**
- Median earnings differ across both gender and ethnicity, with measurable pay gaps persisting across several demographic groups.
